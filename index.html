Current Projects

Ongoing explorations on GitHub. For papers, see Research.

U.S. Labor Share of GDP: Methodology and Measurement
Work in Progress

How should the labor share be measured? This note examines methodological debates around proprietor income allocation, depreciation treatment, and sectoral adjustments that significantly affect labor share trends.

AI and GitHub's Growing Power Law of Innovation
Work in Progress

Is AI-assisted coding concentrating software development among fewer contributors? Analysis of commit patterns across 6 years of GH Archive data (2019-2025) to test whether AI tools amplify or flatten the distribution of developer productivity.

AI-Web Content Ecosystem Simulator
Work in Progress

Interactive agent-based model exploring how AI systems that consume web content affect the incentives for content creation. Simulates feedback loops between AI training, content quality, and creator economics under different policy regimes.

Do Follow-Up Suggestions Extend Engagement?
Work in Progress

Survival analysis of 1M+ ChatGPT conversations from WildChat. Do AI-generated follow-up suggestions extend session duration? What predicts conversation abandonment vs. continuation?

AI Disclosures Project

I co-direct the AI Disclosures Project with Tim O'Reilly, focused on architecting healthier AI markets through research, collaboration, and policy engagement. Key focus areas:

  • Open protocols and interoperability standards for AI
  • SEC disclosure requirements for AI systems
  • AI incident reporting and telemetry
  • Memory interoperability and data portability

Supported by the Alfred P. Sloan Foundation, Omidyar Network, and Patrick J. McGovern Foundation. We convene a Rockefeller Foundation Bellagio conference on AI market standards in April 2026.
